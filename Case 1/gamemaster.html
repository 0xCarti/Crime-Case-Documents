Game Master Brief — Case 1 & Series Overview

Confidential. Do not include in player folders.

Premise

Players are private investigators hired by a third party after police close multiple seemingly unrelated cases. Their goal is to link the cases, establish motive and method, and identify the hidden culprit. The true culprit appears in the documents but is not labeled as a suspect.

Series at a Glance (4–5 Cases)

  • Case 1 — Halden Death: Apparent suicide of real estate magnate Martin Halden. Actually a murder staged by CTO Daniel Cross, motivated by exposure of a fraud scheme (archival hardware & off-books data).
  • Case 2 — Evergreen Antiques Burglary: Mid-level theft with odd item selection. Connects via resale channel and a DVR gap.
  • Case 3 — Warehouse Arson (Fraud Cover-up): Fire destroys storage unit B-17 holding HRG archives. Insurance and IT work orders link to Case 1 vendor trail.
  • Case 4 — Side Case / Red Herring: Looks connected but mostly noise; contains one or two real signals that tie back to Cross (e.g., shared VPN endpoint, shared phone contact).

Win Condition

Players present a coherent hypothesis (method + motive + linkage across cases) and confidently open the envelope naming the culprit: Daniel Cross.

Evidence Index (Case 1)

  • Weapon & Ballistics: .38 revolver; muzzle-to-target 0–3 cm; prints inconclusive on grip; Ward partial on cylinder (innocuous).
  • GSR: Positive on left hand dorsum; inconsistent with right-hand suicide staging.
  • Autopsy/Tox: Low BAC; trace benzo below threshold (possible pre-sedation).
  • Digital: VPN session 21:58–22:03; 4.2 GB purge; vendor “North Prairie Systems” in headers.
  • Call Log: Multiple calls with Cross; call to lawyer; spam/ordinary calls as noise.
  • Paper Trail: Invoice, check stub, ledger entries referencing North Prairie; IT work order signed by Cross.
  • Scene: Torn note fragments; door unlocked; threshold scuff; safe keypad wiped.

Clue Map (GM Eyes Only)

  • Real tells: GSR left-hand heavy; VPN session overlapping TOD; deletions immediately prior to discovery; invoice trail; access logs with Cross at server room.
  • Red herrings: Paint receipt (unrelated), neighbor misattributes gunshot to backfire, media article framing suicide, charity disbursement noise.
  • Pacing: Most strong clues are split between Forensics, Digital, and Financial inserts.

Cross-Case Linkage (for GM)

  • Vendor Thread: “North Prairie Systems” recurs in Case 1 financials and Case 3 warehouse equipment manifests.
  • Access/Timing Thread: DVR gaps in Case 2 align with times Cross is remoted in (same VPN gateway string shows up).
  • Phone/Contact Thread: One common contact appears across CDRs (burner or store number) — reveal in later cases.

What Players Might Miss

  • GSR handedness mismatch if they don’t compare to right/left dominance.
  • Header cache line in Digital Forensics pointing to vendor.
  • Ledger “capitalization adjustment” matching invoice total.

Failsafes / Hints You Can Offer

  • “Compare the hands and the residue.”
  • “Follow the vendor names across documents.”
  • “Why would someone wipe a keypad they own?”
